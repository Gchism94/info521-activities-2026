Covariance ellipses & Fisher information

How tightly does the data pin down the fitted weights? Treat the full-data fit as the true $\mathbf{w}$; the ellipse is the sampling spread of $\hat{\mathbf{w}}$, and resampling fills it. Watch it shrink as $N$ grows and stretch with the noise $\sigma^2$.

$$\mathcal{I} \;=\; \tfrac{1}{\sigma^2}\,\mathbf{X}^{\!\top}\mathbf{X} \qquad \mathrm{cov}\{\hat{\mathbf{w}}\} \;=\; \sigma^2\!\left(\mathbf{X}^{\!\top}\mathbf{X}\right)^{-1} \;=\; \mathcal{I}^{-1}$$
