PCA: the direction that captures the most variance

Each point is a patient — systolic vs. diastolic blood pressure, two correlated measurements. Rotate the axis to find the direction along which the projected data spreads the most; that direction is the first principal component. Watch how much of the total variance a single component keeps, and how the reconstruction error shrinks as you approach it. (PCA works on the data centered at its mean.)

$$\mathrm{Var}(\mathbf{u}^\top \mathbf{x}) = \mathbf{u}^\top \boldsymbol{\Sigma}\, \mathbf{u}$$
maximized over unit $\mathbf{u}$ by the top eigenvector of $\boldsymbol{\Sigma}$
$$\boldsymbol{\Sigma}\,\mathbf{u}_k = \lambda_k \mathbf{u}_k \qquad \text{variance explained}_k = \dfrac{\lambda_k}{\sum_j \lambda_j}$$
